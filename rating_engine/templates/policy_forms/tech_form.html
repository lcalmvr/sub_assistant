Section I - Insuring Agreements
A. Technology Errors & Omissions

The Insurer shall pay on behalf of the Insured all Loss and Claim Expenses that the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay as a result of a Claim first made against the Insured during the Policy Period for a Technology Wrongful Act arising from:

  1. Any actual or alleged act, error, omission, neglect, or breach of duty in the performance of Technology Services for others;
  2. Failure of Technology Products to perform as intended, represented, or warranted;
  3. Failure to meet service level agreements or contractual performance standards;
  4. Negligent implementation, integration, customization, or maintenance of software, systems, or technology solutions;
  5. Providing inaccurate, incomplete, or negligent technology advice, recommendations, or consulting services;
  6. Failure to deliver Technology Products or Technology Services in accordance with agreed specifications or requirements;
  7. Infringement of intellectual property rights in the performance of Technology Services or delivery of Technology Products (excluding patent infringement).
B. Indemnification of Clients

The Insurer shall pay on behalf of the Insured all Loss and Claim Expenses that the Insured becomes legally obligated to indemnify a client for, pursuant to a written contract, arising from a Technology Wrongful Act in connection with Technology Products or Technology Services provided to such client.

C. Rectification Costs

The Insurer shall pay the Insured for Rectification Costs incurred by the Insured to correct, repair, or re-perform Technology Products or Technology Services that fail to meet contractual requirements, provided such failure would otherwise give rise to a covered Claim.

Section II - Definitions

"Claim" means a written demand for monetary damages or non-monetary relief, including any civil proceeding commenced by service of a complaint or similar pleading, arbitration proceeding, or mediation.

"Claim Expenses" means reasonable fees, costs, and expenses incurred by or on behalf of the Insured in the investigation, defense, or appeal of a Claim, including attorney's fees, expert witness fees, and court costs.

"Insured" means the Named Insured and any subsidiary or affiliated entity in which the Named Insured holds more than 50% ownership interest.

"Loss" means damages, settlements, and judgments that the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay as a result of a Claim, including pre-judgment and post-judgment interest.

"Policy Period" means the period from the effective date to the expiration date as stated in the Declarations.

"Rectification Costs" means reasonable costs incurred by the Insured to correct, repair, or re-perform Technology Products or Technology Services, including labor costs and third-party contractor fees.

"Technology Products" means any software, hardware, firmware, systems, applications, platforms, or technology solutions designed, developed, manufactured, sold, licensed, leased, distributed, or supported by the Insured, including custom software, SaaS platforms, mobile applications, and APIs.

"Technology Services" means any technology-related services provided by the Insured to others for a fee, including but not limited to:

  1. Software development, customization, and maintenance;
  2. System integration and implementation;
  3. IT consulting and advisory services;
  4. Managed IT services and outsourcing;
  5. Cloud computing and hosting services;
  6. Data processing and analytics;
  7. Technology support and help desk services;
  8. Network design and management;
  9. Quality assurance and testing services.

"Technology Wrongful Act" means any actual or alleged act, error, omission, neglect, misstatement, misleading statement, or breach of duty in connection with the design, development, manufacture, sale, licensing, distribution, implementation, integration, maintenance, support, or performance of Technology Products or Technology Services.

Section III - Exclusions

This Policy does not apply to any Claim, Loss, or expense:

  1. Prior Acts. Based upon any Technology Wrongful Act occurring prior to the Retroactive Date stated in the Declarations, if any.
  2. Prior Knowledge. Based upon facts or circumstances known to any Executive Officer of the Insured prior to Policy inception that could reasonably be expected to give rise to a Claim.
  3. Prior Claims. Based upon any claim, suit, or proceeding pending or threatened prior to the Policy Period.
  4. Intentional Acts. Arising from any dishonest, fraudulent, criminal, or malicious act by an Insured; provided this exclusion shall not apply unless established by final adjudication.
  5. Bodily Injury / Property Damage. For bodily injury, sickness, disease, death, or physical damage to or destruction of tangible property.
  6. Employment Practices. Arising from any employment-related claim.
  7. Patent Infringement. Arising from any claim of patent infringement or misappropriation of trade secrets.
  8. Contractual Liability. Arising from any liability assumed under contract beyond the liability that would exist absent such contract, except for indemnification obligations covered under Insuring Agreement B.
  9. Product Recall. For costs to recall, withdraw, repair, replace, or dispose of Technology Products.
  10. Cost Overruns. For cost overruns, budget disputes, or failure to deliver Technology Products or Technology Services on time or within budget.
  11. Profit/Revenue Guarantees. For failure to meet profit, revenue, cost savings, or return on investment guarantees or projections.
  12. Pricing Disputes. For disputes over fees, pricing, or billing for Technology Products or Technology Services.
  13. Intentional Non-Performance. For deliberate refusal to perform contractual obligations or abandonment of a project.
  14. Securities. Arising from securities law violations.
  15. Antitrust. Arising from antitrust or competition law violations.
  16. Insured vs. Insured. Brought by or on behalf of any Insured against another Insured.
Section IV - General Conditions
1. Policy Period and Territory

This Policy applies to Claims first made and reported during the Policy Period for Technology Wrongful Acts occurring anywhere in the world. Coverage applies to Claims brought in any jurisdiction.

2. Claims-Made Coverage

This is a claims-made policy. Coverage applies only to Claims first made against the Insured and reported to the Insurer during the Policy Period or any applicable Extended Reporting Period.

3. Limits of Liability

The Aggregate Limit stated in the Declarations is the maximum amount the Insurer shall pay for all Claims during the Policy Period. Defense costs and Claim Expenses are included within and reduce the Aggregate Limit.

4. Retention

The Retention stated in the Declarations applies to each Claim. The Insured shall be responsible for all Loss and Claim Expenses within the Retention before the Insurer's obligations begin.

5. Other Insurance

This Policy shall be excess over any other valid and collectible insurance available to the Insured, unless such other insurance is specifically written as excess of this Policy.

6. Subrogation

In the event of any payment under this Policy, the Insurer shall be subrogated to all rights of recovery of the Insured.

7. Cancellation

This Policy may be cancelled by the Named Insured at any time. The Insurer may cancel for non-payment upon 10 days notice, or for any other reason upon 60 days notice.

Section V - Claims Conditions
1. Notice of Claim

The Insured shall give written notice to the Insurer as soon as practicable after any Executive Officer becomes aware of a Claim.

2. Notice of Circumstances

If the Insured becomes aware of circumstances that may reasonably give rise to a Claim and provides written notice during the Policy Period, any subsequent Claim arising from such circumstances shall be deemed first made during the Policy Period.

3. Cooperation

The Insured shall cooperate with the Insurer in the investigation, defense, and settlement of Claims. The Insured shall not admit liability or settle without the Insurer's prior written consent.

4. Defense and Settlement

The Insurer shall have the right and duty to defend any Claim. The Insurer may settle any Claim with the consent of the Insured, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.

5. Extended Reporting Period

If this Policy is cancelled or non-renewed, the Named Insured may purchase an Extended Reporting Period within 60 days for Claims first made and reported during the extended period for Technology Wrongful Acts occurring prior to the end of the Policy Period.
