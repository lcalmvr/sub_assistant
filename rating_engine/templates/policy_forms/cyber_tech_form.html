Section I - Insuring Agreements
{# All Cyber coverages #}
A. Network Security & Privacy Liability

The Insurer shall pay on behalf of the Insured all Loss and Claim Expenses that the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay as a result of a Claim first made against the Insured during the Policy Period for a Wrongful Act arising from:

  1. Failure to prevent unauthorized access to or use of a Computer System;
  2. Failure to prevent a denial of service attack against a Computer System;
  3. Failure to prevent the transmission of Malicious Code from a Computer System to third parties;
  4. Failure to prevent unauthorized access to, disclosure of, or use of Personally Identifiable Information or Protected Health Information;
  5. Failure to comply with the Insured's privacy policy as published on the Insured's website.
B. Privacy Regulatory Proceedings

The Insurer shall pay on behalf of the Insured all Regulatory Fines and Penalties and Claim Expenses that the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay as a result of a Regulatory Proceeding first made during the Policy Period arising from an actual or alleged violation of Privacy Regulations.

C. Payment Card Industry (PCI) Liability

The Insurer shall pay on behalf of the Insured all PCI Fines and Assessments and Claim Expenses that the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay as a result of a PCI Claim first made during the Policy Period arising from an actual or alleged violation of PCI Data Security Standards.

D. Media Liability

The Insurer shall pay on behalf of the Insured all Loss and Claim Expenses that the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay as a result of a Claim first made during the Policy Period for a Media Wrongful Act in content created or disseminated by the Insured.

E. Business Interruption

The Insurer shall pay the Insured for Business Interruption Loss sustained by the Insured during the Policy Period as a direct result of a total or partial interruption of the Insured's business caused by a Security Failure that first occurs during the Policy Period.

F. System Failure

The Insurer shall pay the Insured for Business Interruption Loss sustained by the Insured during the Policy Period as a direct result of a total or partial interruption of the Insured's business caused by an unintentional or unplanned outage of a Computer System that first occurs during the Policy Period.

G. Dependent Business Interruption

The Insurer shall pay the Insured for Business Interruption Loss sustained by the Insured during the Policy Period as a direct result of a Security Failure or System Failure of a Service Provider's computer system upon which the Insured relies for its business operations.

H. Cyber Extortion

The Insurer shall pay the Insured for Extortion Expenses incurred by the Insured during the Policy Period as a direct result of an Extortion Threat first made during the Policy Period.

I. Data Recovery

The Insurer shall pay the Insured for Data Recovery Expenses incurred by the Insured during the Policy Period to restore, recreate, or recollect Digital Assets that are lost, corrupted, or destroyed as a direct result of a Security Failure that first occurs during the Policy Period.

J. Reputational Harm

The Insurer shall pay the Insured for Crisis Management Expenses incurred by the Insured during the Policy Period to mitigate adverse publicity arising from a Security Failure or Privacy Breach first occurring during the Policy Period.

{# Tech E&O coverage - additional to cyber #}
K. Technology Errors & Omissions

The Insurer shall pay on behalf of the Insured all Loss and Claim Expenses that the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay as a result of a Claim first made against the Insured during the Policy Period for a Technology Wrongful Act arising from:

  1. Any actual or alleged act, error, omission, neglect, or breach of duty in the performance of Technology Services for others;
  2. Failure of Technology Products to perform as intended or warranted;
  3. Failure to meet service level agreements or contractual performance standards;
  4. Negligent implementation, integration, customization, or maintenance of software or systems;
  5. Providing inaccurate or incomplete technology advice or recommendations.
Section II - Definitions
{# All cyber definitions plus Tech E&O specific #}

"Business Interruption Loss" means the net income that would have been earned or incurred by the Insured during the Period of Restoration, plus continuing normal operating expenses incurred, including payroll.

"Claim" means a written demand for monetary damages or non-monetary relief, including any civil proceeding commenced by service of a complaint or similar pleading, arbitration proceeding, or mediation.

"Claim Expenses" means reasonable fees, costs, and expenses incurred by or on behalf of the Insured in the investigation, defense, or appeal of a Claim.

"Computer System" means any computer hardware, software, firmware, and network infrastructure, including cloud computing environments and hosted systems, owned, operated, leased, or under the control of the Insured.

"Digital Assets" means electronic data and software stored on a Computer System, including databases, source code, and digital records.

"Extortion Threat" means a credible threat to release confidential data, introduce Malicious Code, render systems inaccessible, or conduct denial of service attacks.

"Insured" means the Named Insured and any subsidiary or affiliated entity in which the Named Insured holds more than 50% ownership interest.

"Loss" means damages, settlements, and judgments that the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay as a result of a Claim.

"Malicious Code" means any code designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to Computer Systems.

"Media Wrongful Act" means defamation, invasion of privacy, misappropriation of likeness, or infringement of copyright or trademark in content created by the Insured.

"Personally Identifiable Information" means any information that can be used to identify an individual, including name, Social Security number, financial account information, or medical information.

"Policy Period" means the period from the effective date to the expiration date as stated in the Declarations.

"Security Failure" means unauthorized access to or use of a Computer System, denial of service attack, transmission of Malicious Code, or unauthorized disclosure of Personally Identifiable Information.

"Service Provider" means a third party that provides technology services to the Insured pursuant to a written contract.

{# Tech E&O specific definitions #}

"Technology Products" means any software, hardware, firmware, systems, or technology solutions designed, developed, manufactured, sold, licensed, leased, or distributed by the Insured.

"Technology Services" means any technology-related services provided by the Insured to others for a fee, including software development, system integration, IT consulting, managed services, cloud services, data processing, and technology support.

"Technology Wrongful Act" means any actual or alleged act, error, omission, neglect, misstatement, or breach of duty in connection with the design, development, sale, licensing, or performance of Technology Products or Technology Services.

"Wrongful Act" means any actual or alleged act, error, omission, or breach of duty giving rise to liability under any of the Insuring Agreements.

Section III - Exclusions

This Policy does not apply to any Claim, Loss, or expense:

  1. Prior Acts. Based upon any Wrongful Act occurring prior to the Retroactive Date.
  2. Prior Knowledge. Based upon facts known to the Insured prior to Policy inception.
  3. Prior Claims. Based upon any claim pending prior to the Policy Period.
  4. Intentional Acts. Arising from dishonest, fraudulent, or criminal conduct.
  5. Bodily Injury / Property Damage. For bodily injury or physical property damage.
  6. Employment Practices. Arising from employment-related claims.
  7. ERISA / Benefits. Arising from employee benefit plans.
  8. Contractual Liability. Assumed under contract beyond ordinary liability.
  9. Patent Infringement. Arising from patent infringement claims.
  10. Securities. Arising from securities law violations.
  11. Antitrust. Arising from antitrust or competition law violations.
  12. War and Terrorism. Arising from war or acts of terrorism (excluding cyber terrorism).
  13. Infrastructure Failure. Arising from utility or infrastructure failures not controlled by Insured.
  14. Product Recall. For costs to recall, repair, or replace Technology Products.
  15. Breach of Contract. For breach of contract unless liability would exist absent the contract.
  16. Cost Overruns. For cost overruns, budget disputes, or failure to deliver on time.
  17. Profit/Revenue Guarantees. For failure to meet profit, revenue, or ROI guarantees.
Section IV - General Conditions
1. Policy Period and Territory

This Policy applies to Claims first made during the Policy Period for Wrongful Acts occurring anywhere in the world.

2. Claims-Made Coverage

This is a claims-made policy. Coverage applies only to Claims first made against the Insured and reported to the Insurer during the Policy Period.

3. Limits of Liability

The Aggregate Limit is the maximum the Insurer shall pay for all Claims during the Policy Period. Defense costs are included within and reduce the Aggregate Limit.

4. Retention

The Retention applies to each Claim. The Insured is responsible for all amounts within the Retention before the Insurer's payment obligations begin.

5. Other Insurance

This Policy is excess over any other valid insurance available to the Insured.

6. Subrogation

The Insurer shall be subrogated to all rights of recovery of the Insured.

7. Cancellation

This Policy may be cancelled by the Insured at any time. The Insurer may cancel for non-payment upon 10 days notice, or for any other reason upon 60 days notice.

Section V - Claims Conditions
1. Notice of Claim

The Insured shall give written notice as soon as practicable after becoming aware of a Claim.

2. Notice of Circumstances

If the Insured becomes aware of circumstances that may give rise to a Claim and provides notice during the Policy Period, any subsequent Claim shall be deemed first made during the Policy Period.

3. Cooperation

The Insured shall cooperate with the Insurer in the investigation, defense, and settlement of Claims. The Insured shall not admit liability or settle without the Insurer's consent.

4. Defense and Settlement

The Insurer has the right and duty to defend Claims. The Insurer may settle with the Insured's consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.
