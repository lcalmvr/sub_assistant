{# Cyber Policy Form Content #} {# Covers: Network Security, Privacy, Regulatory, Media, BI, Extortion, Data Recovery #}
Section I - Insuring Agreements
A. Network Security & Privacy Liability

The Insurer shall pay on behalf of the Insured all Loss and Claim Expenses that the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay as a result of a Claim first made against the Insured during the Policy Period for a Wrongful Act arising from:

  1. Failure to prevent unauthorized access to or use of a Computer System;
  2. Failure to prevent a denial of service attack against a Computer System;
  3. Failure to prevent the transmission of Malicious Code from a Computer System to third parties;
  4. Failure to prevent unauthorized access to, disclosure of, or use of Personally Identifiable Information or Protected Health Information;
  5. Failure to comply with the Insured's privacy policy as published on the Insured's website.
B. Privacy Regulatory Proceedings

The Insurer shall pay on behalf of the Insured all Regulatory Fines and Penalties and Claim Expenses that the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay as a result of a Regulatory Proceeding first made during the Policy Period arising from an actual or alleged violation of Privacy Regulations.

C. Payment Card Industry (PCI) Liability

The Insurer shall pay on behalf of the Insured all PCI Fines and Assessments and Claim Expenses that the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay as a result of a PCI Claim first made during the Policy Period arising from an actual or alleged violation of PCI Data Security Standards.

D. Media Liability

The Insurer shall pay on behalf of the Insured all Loss and Claim Expenses that the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay as a result of a Claim first made during the Policy Period for a Media Wrongful Act in content created or disseminated by the Insured.

E. Business Interruption

The Insurer shall pay the Insured for Business Interruption Loss sustained by the Insured during the Policy Period as a direct result of a total or partial interruption of the Insured's business caused by a Security Failure that first occurs during the Policy Period.

F. System Failure

The Insurer shall pay the Insured for Business Interruption Loss sustained by the Insured during the Policy Period as a direct result of a total or partial interruption of the Insured's business caused by an unintentional or unplanned outage of a Computer System that first occurs during the Policy Period.

G. Dependent Business Interruption

The Insurer shall pay the Insured for Business Interruption Loss sustained by the Insured during the Policy Period as a direct result of a Security Failure or System Failure of a Service Provider's computer system upon which the Insured relies for its business operations.

H. Cyber Extortion

The Insurer shall pay the Insured for Extortion Expenses incurred by the Insured during the Policy Period as a direct result of an Extortion Threat first made during the Policy Period. Coverage includes ransomware demands, threats to release confidential data, and denial of service threats.

I. Data Recovery

The Insurer shall pay the Insured for Data Recovery Expenses incurred by the Insured during the Policy Period to restore, recreate, or recollect Digital Assets that are lost, corrupted, or destroyed as a direct result of a Security Failure that first occurs during the Policy Period.

J. Reputational Harm

The Insurer shall pay the Insured for Crisis Management Expenses incurred by the Insured during the Policy Period to mitigate adverse publicity arising from a Security Failure or Privacy Breach first occurring during the Policy Period.

Section II - Definitions

"Business Interruption Loss" means the net income that would have been earned or incurred by the Insured during the Period of Restoration, plus continuing normal operating expenses incurred, including payroll.

"Claim" means a written demand for monetary damages or non-monetary relief, including any civil proceeding commenced by service of a complaint or similar pleading, arbitration proceeding, or mediation.

"Claim Expenses" means reasonable fees, costs, and expenses incurred by or on behalf of the Insured in the investigation, defense, or appeal of a Claim, including attorney's fees, expert witness fees, and court costs.

"Computer System" means any computer hardware, software, firmware, and network infrastructure, including cloud computing environments and hosted systems, owned, operated, leased, or under the control of the Insured.

"Crisis Management Expenses" means reasonable fees and expenses for public relations, crisis communications, credit monitoring services, call center services, and forensic investigation services.

"Data Recovery Expenses" means reasonable and necessary costs to restore, recreate, or recollect Digital Assets, including the cost of data recovery specialists and replacement media.

"Digital Assets" means electronic data and software stored on a Computer System, including databases, source code, and digital records.

"Extortion Expenses" means: (a) money, cryptocurrency, or other consideration paid to terminate an Extortion Threat; and (b) reasonable fees for a professional negotiator.

"Extortion Threat" means a credible threat to: (a) release, divulge, or publicize Personally Identifiable Information or confidential business information; (b) introduce Malicious Code; (c) render Computer Systems inaccessible through encryption (ransomware); or (d) conduct a denial of service attack.

"Insured" means the Named Insured and any subsidiary or affiliated entity in which the Named Insured holds more than 50% ownership interest.

"Loss" means damages, settlements, and judgments that the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay as a result of a Claim, including pre-judgment and post-judgment interest.

"Malicious Code" means any code or software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to Computer Systems, including viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware, and spyware.

"Media Wrongful Act" means defamation, libel, slander, invasion of privacy, misappropriation of likeness, or infringement of copyright, trademark, or trade dress in content created or disseminated by the Insured.

"PCI Data Security Standards" means the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard as published by the PCI Security Standards Council.

"Period of Restoration" means the period beginning when a Security Failure or System Failure first occurs and ending when the Computer System is restored to substantially the same condition as before, or 180 days, whichever is earlier.

"Personally Identifiable Information" means any information that can be used to identify an individual, including name, Social Security number, financial account information, medical information, biometric data, or login credentials.

"Policy Period" means the period from the effective date to the expiration date as stated in the Declarations.

"Privacy Regulations" means any federal, state, or foreign statute or regulation governing the collection, use, disclosure, or protection of personal information, including CCPA, GDPR, HIPAA, and state data breach notification laws.

"Regulatory Proceeding" means any administrative or regulatory proceeding brought by a governmental authority regarding an alleged violation of Privacy Regulations.

"Security Failure" means: (a) unauthorized access to or use of a Computer System; (b) denial of service attack; (c) transmission of Malicious Code; or (d) theft, loss, or unauthorized disclosure of Personally Identifiable Information.

"Service Provider" means a third party that provides information technology services, cloud computing, data hosting, or other technology services to the Insured pursuant to a written contract.

"Wrongful Act" means any actual or alleged act, error, omission, or breach of duty giving rise to liability under the Insuring Agreements.

Section III - Exclusions

This Policy does not apply to any Claim, Loss, or expense:

  1. Prior Acts. Based upon, arising from, or in any way related to any Wrongful Act occurring prior to the Retroactive Date stated in the Declarations, if any.
  2. Prior Knowledge. Based upon facts or circumstances known to any Executive Officer of the Insured prior to the Policy Period inception date that could reasonably be expected to give rise to a Claim.
  3. Prior Claims or Litigation. Based upon, arising from, or in any way related to any claim, suit, or proceeding pending or threatened against the Insured prior to the Policy Period, or any fact, circumstance, or situation underlying such claim.
  4. Intentional Acts. Arising from any dishonest, fraudulent, criminal, or malicious act by an Insured; however, this exclusion shall not apply unless such conduct is established by a final adjudication or admission.
  5. Bodily Injury / Property Damage. For bodily injury, sickness, disease, death, or physical damage to or destruction of tangible property, including loss of use.
  6. Employment Practices. Arising from any employment-related claim, including wrongful termination, discrimination, harassment, or retaliation.
  7. ERISA / Benefits. Arising from any claim related to employee benefit plans governed by ERISA.
  8. Professional Services. Arising from the rendering or failure to render professional services for others for a fee; however, this exclusion shall not apply to Network Security & Privacy Liability coverage.
  9. Contractual Liability. Arising from any liability assumed under any contract or agreement, except to the extent the Insured would be liable in the absence of such contract.
  10. Intellectual Property. Arising from any claim of patent infringement or misappropriation of trade secrets; however, this exclusion shall not apply to Media Liability coverage for copyright, trademark, or trade dress infringement.
  11. Securities. Arising from any claim related to securities, including violations of securities laws, insider trading, or shareholder derivative actions.
  12. Antitrust / Competition. Arising from any claim alleging violations of antitrust, competition, or unfair trade practices laws.
  13. War and Terrorism. Arising from war, invasion, acts of foreign enemies, hostilities, civil war, rebellion, revolution, insurrection, or acts of terrorism; however, this exclusion shall not apply to cyber terrorism.
  14. Government Actions. Arising from any action by or on behalf of any government to nationalize, confiscate, or requisition property of the Insured.
  15. Nuclear / Radioactive. Arising from nuclear reaction, radiation, or radioactive contamination.
  16. Infrastructure Failure. Arising from the failure of electrical power, telecommunications, gas, water, or other infrastructure not under the control of the Insured.
  17. Unsolicited Communications. Arising from the sending of unsolicited emails, faxes, or other communications in violation of TCPA, CAN-SPAM, or similar laws.
  18. Betterment. For any costs to improve or upgrade Computer Systems or security measures beyond the condition that existed prior to a Security Failure.
Section IV - General Conditions
1. Policy Period and Territory

This Policy applies to Claims first made and reported during the Policy Period for Wrongful Acts occurring anywhere in the world. Coverage applies to Claims brought in any jurisdiction.

2. Claims-Made Coverage

This is a claims-made policy. Coverage applies only to Claims first made against the Insured and reported to the Insurer during the Policy Period or any applicable Extended Reporting Period.

3. Limits of Liability

The Aggregate Limit stated in the Declarations is the maximum amount the Insurer shall pay for all Claims during the Policy Period, regardless of the number of Claims, Insureds, or Wrongful Acts. Defense costs and Claim Expenses are included within and reduce the Aggregate Limit and applicable sublimits.

4. Retention

The Retention stated in the Declarations applies to each Claim. The Insured shall be responsible for all Loss, Claim Expenses, and other covered amounts within the Retention. The Retention must be satisfied before the Insurer's payment obligations begin.

5. Other Insurance

This Policy shall be excess over any other valid and collectible insurance available to the Insured, unless such other insurance is specifically written as excess of this Policy.

6. Subrogation

In the event of any payment under this Policy, the Insurer shall be subrogated to all rights of recovery of the Insured against any person or entity. The Insured shall execute all documents necessary to secure such rights and shall do nothing to prejudice such rights.

7. Assignment

This Policy may not be assigned without the prior written consent of the Insurer.

8. Entire Agreement

This Policy, together with the Declarations, endorsements, and the application (which is incorporated by reference), constitutes the entire agreement between the parties. No agent has authority to change or waive any provision of this Policy.

9. Cancellation

This Policy may be cancelled by the Named Insured at any time by written notice to the Insurer. The Insurer may cancel this Policy for non-payment of premium upon 10 days written notice, or for any other reason upon 60 days written notice to the Named Insured.

10. Representations and Severability

The Insured represents that the statements in the application are true and complete. No knowledge or information possessed by any Insured shall be imputed to any other Insured for purposes of determining coverage.

Section V - Claims Conditions
1. Notice of Claim

The Insured shall give written notice to the Insurer as soon as practicable after any Executive Officer becomes aware of a Claim, but in no event later than sixty (60) days after the end of the Policy Period or any Extended Reporting Period.

2. Notice of Circumstances

If during the Policy Period the Insured becomes aware of circumstances which may reasonably be expected to give rise to a Claim, and gives written notice to the Insurer during the Policy Period describing such circumstances in detail, then any Claim subsequently arising from such circumstances shall be deemed to have been first made during the Policy Period.

3. Cooperation

The Insured shall cooperate with the Insurer in the investigation, defense, and settlement of any Claim. The Insured shall provide all information, documents, and assistance reasonably requested by the Insurer. The Insured shall not admit liability, make any payment, assume any obligation, or settle any Claim without the prior written consent of the Insurer.

4. Defense and Settlement

The Insurer shall have the right and duty to defend any Claim, even if the allegations are groundless, false, or fraudulent. The Insurer may select defense counsel. The Insurer shall have the right to settle any Claim with the consent of the Insured, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld.

5. Hammer Clause

If the Insured refuses to consent to a settlement recommended by the Insurer, the Insurer's liability shall not exceed the amount for which the Claim could have been settled, plus Claim Expenses incurred up to the date of such refusal.

6. Extended Reporting Period

If this Policy is cancelled or non-renewed, the Named Insured may purchase an Extended Reporting Period within 60 days of cancellation or non-renewal. The Extended Reporting Period provides coverage for Claims first made and reported during the extended period for Wrongful Acts occurring prior to the end of the Policy Period.
